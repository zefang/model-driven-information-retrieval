ETA News Release: [July 3, 2003]
Contact Name: Elissa Pruett

Labor Grant Will Aid Dislocated South Dakota Workers
Secretary of Labor Elaine L. Chao Announces National Emergency Grant of $123,694 to Supplement Re-employment Programs

WASHINGTON--U.S. Secretary of Labor Elaine L. Chao announced a National Emergency Grant of $123,694 that will aid South Dakota's trade certified workers. National Emergency Grants are part of the secretary's discretionary fund. A grant is awarded after a state submits a request and the grant guidelines have been met.

"The President and I are committed to helping displaced workers access the job and skills training they need to find new jobs that will enable them to provide for themselves and their families," Chao said. "We hope these funds will be deployed as quickly as possible to help these South Dakota workers."

The grant project delivers critical help to dislocated South Dakota workers, underscoring one of the main goals of the President's economic agenda. The project will provide a variety of transition-related assistance including assessment, counseling, training, job development, job placement and supportive services.

"Helping all American workers who have lost their jobs remains a top priority for this Administration," said Chao.

For more information on the Department of Labor's unemployment and re-employment programs please go to http://www.dol.gov.
